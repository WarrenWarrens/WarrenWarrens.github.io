FLIC: Feedback for Live Interactive Coding

Firefox extension and website, that allows professors to stream their screen to an audience via room code. Viewers can interact with the stream, by placing markers and comments on the screen that appear in the exact same position on the professors screen.

Created as my Honours Thesis project at Ontario Tech and supervised by Dr. Eric Rapos & Dr. Michael Miljanovic. FLIC aims to bridge the gap of communication between professors & students during CS lectures, by allowing precise, anonomyous and direct communication via markers that signal different intentions or comments precisely on a professors screen.

Key Features

  • Score ceiling that increments downwards
  • Card animations
  • Economy that rewards scoring at or just under the ceiling.
  • In game store to purchase cards
